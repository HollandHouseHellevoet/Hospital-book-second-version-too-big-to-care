# OUTLINE.md — Too Big To Care

## Subtitle
How Health Systems Hijacked Healthcare

## Thesis
The top health systems — academic, nonprofit, and for-profit — consolidated into regional monopolies, vertically integrated into insurance, captured their trade associations and political committees, and extracted wealth under the guise of care. The payers copied the playbook. The systems built it.

## Chapter 12: The Prescription

### Beats

**I. What Congress Should Do (But Won't)**
- Site-neutral payments
- CON repeal (federal incentive to states)
- Stark Law / physician ownership ban repeal
- 340B reform (tie to actual charity care)
- Price transparency enforcement with teeth

*Punchline: These policies are obvious. They've been proposed for decades. They don't pass because the cartel owns the gatekeepers.*

**II. What the Market Needs**
- Healthcare Commodities Exchange — price discovery for the unpriced economy
- Surgery futures and options (Dutch's 2010 thesis)
- Transparent, tradeable pricing that breaks the opacity

*Punchline: Healthcare will remain extractive until it gets priced. The exchange is the infrastructure.*

**III. What You Can Do Now**
- **For physicians:** Exit employment. Build independent practices. Join coalitions (MedMerge). Post your prices. Build your audience.
- **For employers:** Direct contract with physicians and ASCs. Bypass the system. Stop funding your own extraction.
- **For patients:** Demand prices before procedures. Use independent facilities. Understand the game.

*Punchline: The system won't save you. Exit it.*
