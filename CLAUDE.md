## Thesis

The top health systems — academic, nonprofit, and for-profit — consolidated into regional monopolies, vertically integrated into insurance, captured their trade associations and political committees, and extracted wealth under the guise of care. The payers copied the playbook. The systems built it.

## Voice & Style

- Contrarian, not academic
- Declarative sentences — no hedging
- Short paragraphs — scannable
- Evidence-heavy, receipts-based
- Accessible to general reader, credible to industry insider
- Tone: Controlled outrage. Not ranting. Prosecutorial.

**Good example:**
> "The trade was supposed to be simple: Nonprofit hospitals get tax exemptions. In return, they serve their communities. $125 billion in tax exemptions. Zero accountability."

**Bad example:**
> "One might argue that nonprofit hospitals perhaps should consider whether their tax exemptions are being used appropriately..."

## Hard Constraints

1. **Never use "provider"** — always "physician" or "doctor"
2. **Never use "should"** in assertions — use declarative statements
3. **No surgical imagery** — no operating tables, scalpels, bloody scenes
4. **All output as markdown (.md)** — never Word, PDF, or HTML unless explicitly requested
5. **No hedge words** — avoid "might," "perhaps," "some argue," "it could be said"
6. **Cite sources** — every claim needs a receipt (data source, article, filing, testimony)
