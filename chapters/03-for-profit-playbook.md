# Chapter 3: The For-Profit Playbook

> *Same consolidation game. No charity pretense.*

---

## The Thesis

For-profit hospital chains play the same consolidation game as nonprofits — they just don't pretend it's charity. HCA, Tenet, and CHS built the playbook. Private equity perfected it. Steward's collapse showed where it ends.

---

## Section 1: The Big Three

### HCA Healthcare
- Founded by Rick Scott (later Florida Governor, US Senator)
- Largest for-profit system in the US
- $60B+ revenue
- The original rollup

### Tenet Healthcare
- Second largest for-profit
- Repeated scandal cycle
- ASC spinoff (USPI)

### Community Health Systems (CHS)
- Rural hospital acquisitions
- Debt-fueled growth
- Divestiture and decline

---

## Section 2: The Rural Play

- Buy distressed rural hospitals
- Extract what's extractable
- Close or flip
- Community left without care
- Rural hospital closure map

## Section 3: Private Equity Enters

- Physician practice rollups
- Staffing companies (Envision, TeamHealth)
- Specialty hospitals
- The surprise billing machine
- PE playbook: leverage, extract, exit

---

## Section 4: The Steward Collapse

- Cerberus Capital acquisition
- Asset stripping
- Real estate sale-leasebacks
- Bankruptcy filing
- Communities scrambling for coverage
- The PE endgame exposed

---

## Section 5: For-Profit vs Nonprofit — Same Game?

- Consolidation patterns identical
- Pricing behavior identical
- Executive compensation comparable
- Only difference: tax status and charity theater
