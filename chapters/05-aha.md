# Chapter 5: The American Hospital Association

> *$100 million a year to protect the castle.*

---

## The Thesis

The AHA started as a professional association for hospital superintendents. It became the most powerful healthcare lobbying force in America. Rick Pollack's AHA doesn't represent patients or communities — it represents the systems that extract from them.

---

## Section 1: Origin Story

- Founded 1898
- Original purpose: hospital administrators sharing best practices
- Evolution: professional association → trade group → lobbying machine
- The mission drift no one noticed

## Section 3: What AHA Protects

### Facility Fees
- Same service, higher price in hospital setting
- AHA fights site-neutral payments

### CON Laws
- Certificate of Need as competition barrier
- AHA opposes CON reform

### 340B Program
- Drug discount program captured by large systems
- AHA defends unlimited 340B expansion

### Physician Ownership Ban (Section 6001)
- AHA supported the ban in ACA
- Keeps physician-owned hospitals from competing

### Tax Exemptions
- $125B in nonprofit exemptions
- AHA resists charity care accountability

---

## Section 4: What AHA Blocks

### Site-Neutral Payments
- Would equalize prices across settings
- AHA's top legislative priority: kill it

### Price Transparency Enforcement
- CMS rules exist, compliance is weak
- AHA fights enforcement with teeth

### Competition
- Every AHA position protects incumbents
- "Patient safety" as cover for protectionism
