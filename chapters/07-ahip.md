# Chapter 7: AHIP and the Payer Side

> *The insurers have their own trade group. Sometimes they fight the hospitals. Usually they don't.*

---

## The Thesis

AHIP represents the health insurers — the entities Americans love to hate. But AHIP and AHA share more interests than they admit. Both benefit from opacity. Both resist transparency. Both profit from a system patients can't navigate. The lobbying ecosystem is less "payers vs providers" and more "incumbents vs everyone else."

---

## Section 1: What Is AHIP?

- America's Health Insurance Plans
- The health insurance trade association
- Members: UnitedHealthcare, Anthem/Elevance, Cigna, Aetna/CVS, Humana, etc.
- Matt Eyles, CEO since 2018

## Section 3: AHIP + AHA — Where They Coordinate

### Against Transparency
- Both resist price transparency enforcement
- Opacity benefits both sides
- Negotiated rates are trade secrets

### Against Competition
- Both support barriers to entry
- New insurers threaten AHIP members
- New hospitals threaten AHA members
- Mutual interest in protecting incumbents

### The Blame Game
- AHIP blames hospitals for high prices
- AHA blames insurers for denials
- Both benefit from the confusion
- Patients can't figure out who's extracting

---

## Section 4: AHIP vs AHA — Where They Fight

### Payment Rates
- AHIP wants lower hospital payments
- AHA wants higher payments
- This is the real negotiation

### Prior Authorization
- AHA blames insurers for delays
- AHIP defends "utilization management"
- Patients suffer either way

### Surprise Billing
- Both tried to shift blame
- No Surprises Act forced arbitration
- Neither side wanted transparency

## Section 5: The PBM Overlay

- Pharmacy Benefit Managers: CVS Caremark, Express Scripts, Optum Rx
- Owned by the big insurers
- Another layer of extraction
- AHIP defends PBM practices
- The vertical integration on the payer side

## Section 6: The Ecosystem

- AHA + FAH + AHIP + PhRMA
- The four pillars of healthcare lobbying
- Sometimes they fight
- When reform threatens all of them, they align
- The patient has no lobby
