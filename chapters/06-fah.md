# Chapter 6: The Federation of American Hospitals

> *The for-profit trade group. Different members, same agenda.*

---

## The Thesis

FAH represents the for-profit hospital chains — HCA, Tenet, CHS, UHS. They don't pretend to be charities. But on the policies that matter — blocking competition, protecting facility fees, fighting transparency — FAH and AHA are aligned. Competition or cartel?

---

## Section 1: What Is FAH?

- Federation of American Hospitals
- The for-profit hospital trade association
- Membership: HCA, Tenet, CHS, UHS, and others
- Smaller than AHA, focused on for-profit interests

---

## Section 2: Chip Kahn

- CEO since 2001
- Former House Ways & Means staffer
- The revolving door personified
- Two decades running FAH's lobbying operation

---

## Section 3: FAH vs AHA — Where They Align

### Site-Neutral Payments
- Both oppose
- Facility fees benefit all hospitals, for-profit and nonprofit

### Price Transparency
- Both resist enforcement
- Opacity benefits incumbents

### Competition Barriers
- Both support CON (in states where members benefit)
- Both supported physician ownership ban

---

## Section 4: FAH vs AHA — Where They Diverge

### 340B
- Nonprofits benefit most from 340B
- For-profits benefit less
- FAH less invested in defending 340B

### Tax Policy
- AHA protects nonprofit exemptions
- FAH members pay taxes (mostly)
- Different financial incentives

### Rhetoric
- AHA wraps everything in "community benefit"
- FAH more direct about business interests
