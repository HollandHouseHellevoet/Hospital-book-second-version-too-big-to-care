# Chapter 1: The Nonprofit Myth

> "The trade was supposed to be simple: Nonprofit hospitals get tax exemptions. In return, they serve their communities."

---

## The Thesis

$125 billion in annual tax exemptions. Zero accountability. Geographic monopolies aren't a bug — they're the business model. Wealth extraction wrapped in a mission statement.

- The original bargain: tax exemption for charity care

## Section 2: The Numbers

- $125B in annual tax exemptions (source needed)

- Executive compensation at "charitable" institutions

## Section 3: The Monopoly Model

## Section 4: Case Studies

### Bon Secours Richmond
- 340B extraction from Richmond Community Hospital
- $276M in 340B revenue, $100M annual profit
- Cut services at the actual hospital serving the poor
- 81% higher COVID death rate in surrounding census tract

### Providence
- McKinsey "Socrates" scheme
- Debt collection against patients who qualified for charity care
- Washington State AG investigation

### UPMC
- ~70% market control in Pittsburgh
- $26B system
- Tax-exempt status vs community benefit
