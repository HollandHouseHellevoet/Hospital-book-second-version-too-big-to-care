# Chapter 12: The Prescription

> *The system won't save you. Exit it.*

---

## The Thesis

There are three levels of prescription: what Congress could do, what the market needs, and what individuals can do now. Congress won't act — the cartel owns the gatekeepers. The market needs infrastructure that doesn't exist yet. But individuals — physicians, employers, patients — can exit the system today. The prescription is not reform. The prescription is exit.

---

## Part I: What Congress Could Do (But Won't)

### Site-Neutral Payments
- Equalize prices across settings
- A $200 echocardiogram in a physician's office is $1,200 at a hospital outpatient department
- Same doctor, same machine, same patient — different setting
- CBO scores show billions in savings
- AHA's top priority: kill it
- Status: perpetually proposed, never passed

### CON Repeal
- Federal incentive for states to repeal Certificate of Need
- Competition isn't dangerous — monopoly is
- 35 states still have CON laws
- Status: no serious federal effort

### Physician Ownership Ban Repeal
- Section 6001 of the ACA
- 99.9% of Americans don't know physicians are prohibited from owning hospitals
- Physician-owned hospitals outperform on quality metrics
- Status: some legislative interest, no movement

### 340B Reform
- Tie 340B eligibility to actual charity care
- Close the spread trade loophole
- Status: AHA protects it fiercely

### Price Transparency Enforcement
- Rules exist, compliance is weak
- Enforcement with teeth: real fines, public reporting
- Status: slowly improving, still inadequate

**The Punchline:**
These policies are obvious. They've been proposed for decades. They don't pass because the cartel owns the gatekeepers. Don't hold your breath waiting for Washington.

---

## Part II: What the Market Needs

### Healthcare Commodities Exchange (HCX)
- Price discovery for the unpriced economy
- Every treatment and service priced and tradeable
- Futures: lock in tomorrow's price today
- Options: hedge against price volatility
- A healthcare S&P 500

### Why This Matters
- "Healthcare isn't expensive. It's unpriced."
- $4.5 trillion economy with no spot market
- Opacity is the product
- Price discovery breaks the extraction model

### The 2010 Thesis
- Surgery futures and options — patented, trademarked
- Nobody was ready
- 35 years of building toward this
- The infrastructure healthcare never had

### Who Builds It
- Not Congress
- Not the incumbents
- Entrepreneurs, physicians, employers who are tired of extraction
- The market will price what the system won't

**The Punchline:**
Healthcare will remain extractive until it gets priced. The exchange is the infrastructure. It's coming — the question is who builds it.

---

## Part III: What You Can Do Now

### For Physicians

**Exit employment.**
- The employed physician is a captured physician
- Your employer sets your prices, your schedule, your autonomy
- Independence is possible

**Build an independent practice.**
- Direct primary care
- Cash-pay specialty
- ASC partnerships
- The tools exist

**Join a coalition.**
- MedMerge: P&C, benefits, retirement, payroll, payer negotiation
- PHA: advocacy and community
- You don't have to do it alone

**Post your prices.**
- Transparency is a competitive advantage
- Patients will find you

**Build your audience.**
- Social media is free distribution
- Physicians with platforms have power
- The establishment fears physician voices

### For Employers

**Direct contract.**
- Bypass the payer-provider cartel
- Contract directly with surgery centers and independent physicians
- Pay less, get more

**Self-fund intelligently.**
- Stop paying for your own extraction
- Reference-based pricing
- Centers of Excellence
- Transparent pharmacy benefits

**Demand prices.**
- Your employees deserve to know what things cost
- Use the transparency data that now exists
- Fire vendors who won't provide it

### For Patients

**Demand prices before procedures.**
- You have the right to know
- Price transparency rules require hospitals to publish
- Use the data

**Use independent facilities.**
- Surgery centers cost less than hospitals for the same procedures
- Independent physicians aren't captured by systems
- You have choices

**Understand the game.**
- Your "nonprofit" hospital is not a charity
- Your insurer profits from confusion
- The system is designed to extract from you
- Knowledge is the first step to exit

---

## Closing

The system will not reform itself. Congress will not save you. The cartel is too powerful, the incentives too entrenched, the revolving door too well-oiled.

But the cracks are real. Employers are exiting. Physicians are leaving employment. Patients are demanding prices. State reforms are passing. The legitimacy of the "nonprofit" hospital is collapsing.

The prescription is not reform. The prescription is exit.

Too big to care. Too captured to change. Too entrenched to reform.

Exit.
