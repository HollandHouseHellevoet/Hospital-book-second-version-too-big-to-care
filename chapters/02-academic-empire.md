# Chapter 2: The Academic Empire

> *Academic medical centers are anchor institutions. They anchor their communities — in place, unable to escape.*

---

## The Thesis

AMCs hold three monopolies: training, research, and clinical care. They use prestige to justify premium pricing, consume their regions through acquisition, and operate as engines of consolidation disguised as centers of excellence.

---

## Section 1: The Three Monopolies

### Training
- GME funding flows through AMCs
- Residency slots as bottleneck
- "We train the doctors, so we control the supply"

### Research
- NIH funding concentration
- Research prestige as marketing
- The teaching hospital premium

### Clinical Care
- Tertiary/quaternary referral patterns
- "Only we can do the hard cases"
- Regional dominance through complexity claims

## Section 5: Case Studies

### UPMC (Pittsburgh)
- From academic medical center to $26B system
- ~70% market control
- Health plan ownership
- National expansion while dominating home market

### Cleveland Clinic
- Global brand, regional monopoly
- Abu Dhabi, London — and 75% of Northeast Ohio
- "Destination medicine" as strategy

### Mass General Brigham (Partners Healthcare)
- Boston market dominance
- The merger that created a giant
- Premium pricing justified by prestige

### Duke Health
- North Carolina expansion
- Academic core, system ambition
